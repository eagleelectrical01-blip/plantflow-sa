📋 How inspections work in PlantFlow
When does an inspection appear here?
An inspection appears when a Maintenance Manager or Artisan submits one from their phone.
• Artisans: Tap the 📅 Schedule tab → "Start safety inspection" button → tick each item → Submit
• Maintenance Manager: Tap the 📊 Inspect tab → tick items → Submit
When should inspections be done?
• Weekly: General safety walkthrough (fire exits, extinguishers, chemical stores)
• Monthly: Machinery guards, electrical panels, pressure vessels
• After incidents: Any fault report involving machinery or chemicals
• Before hazardous work: Required before a Permit to Work is issued
How results link to OHSA compliance: Each inspection that passes improves the relevant OHSA section score on the OHSA Compliance screen. Failed items create action items that the manager must resolve and record. The more inspections submitted, the more accurate your OHSA scores become.

ℹ How these scores work: Each section below maps to a section of the South African OHSA Act 85 of 1993. The score reflects how well your factory is complying with that section right now. You update the score and notes manually after doing a walkthrough or inspection. Anything below 80% shows as "Review" — you need to take action. The linked job types tell you which PlantFlow job card types contribute to that score. Click "Update" on any row to edit that section's score and notes.
OHSA Compliance by Section
How to improve a low score
Below 70% (Critical): Create a job card immediately, assign to a responsible artisan, and schedule a follow-up inspection within 7 days.
70–80% (Review): Add a PM schedule task for the affected area. The artisan will see it on their phone and submit an inspection report when done.
Above 80% (Good): Maintain with regular PM schedules. Submit inspections monthly to keep the score current.

💳 How billing works — your process
Step 1 — Add the company
Click "+ Add company" above. Enter their name, email, phone, industry, subscription price and trial days. The trial countdown starts immediately from today.
Step 2 — Send them a payment request
Click "📧 Send payment request" on any company row (appears when trial is close to expiring). This opens an email to the client with a PayFast payment link and their subscription details.
Step 3 — Confirm payment
When they pay (EFT, card, or PayFast), open the company detail, set Status to Active — paid, update the Expires date, and save. The trial countdown changes to "✓ Subscription active".
If they don't pay
Click ⏸ Hold on their row. Their PlantFlow login still works but you can see their status is "held" — use this as a flag to follow up. Currently PlantFlow does not block access automatically (you control this manually).
Payment methods you can use
• EFT / bank transfer — send your banking details in the email
• PayFast — paste your PayFast payment link in the email template
• SnapScan — include your SnapScan QR code
• Cash — mark as active manually after receiving
Tip: Set the trial to 30 days so clients have time to test. Follow up on day 25 with a payment request email. After payment, extend their expiry by 30 days and set status to Active.
